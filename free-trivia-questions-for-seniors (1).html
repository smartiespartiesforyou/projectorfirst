40 Free Easy Trivia Questions for Seniors (With Answers)

Good senior trivia has one rule: everyone in the room should be able to answer most questions. The goal isn't to stump anyone — it's to give every resident, including those with memory loss, the feeling of knowing the answer and saying it out loud with the group. These 40 questions are calibrated for that. Read them aloud, project them in large text, or print this page.

Everyday Life

  1. What color is a school bus? (Yellow)
  2. How many days are in a week? (Seven)
  3. What do you use an umbrella for? (Rain)
  4. What meal do most people eat in the morning? (Breakfast)
  5. What do bees make? (Honey)
  6. What season comes after winter? (Spring)
  7. How many pennies make a dollar? (One hundred)
  8. What do you call frozen water? (Ice)
  9. What fruit is famous for keeping the doctor away? (An apple)
  10. What do cows give us to drink? (Milk)

Animals

  1. What animal says "moo"? (A cow)
  2. What is man's best friend? (A dog)
  3. What big gray animal has a trunk? (An elephant)
  4. What bird is the symbol of America? (The bald eagle)
  5. What animal is known as the king of the jungle? (The lion)
  6. What black-and-white bird cannot fly and lives on ice? (A penguin)
  7. What insect makes a web? (A spider)
  8. What animal carries its house on its back? (A turtle or snail)
  9. What farm bird wakes everyone up in the morning? (The rooster)
  10. What do you call a baby cat? (A kitten)

The Good Old Days

  1. What did people use to play records? (A record player / phonograph)
  2. Who was famous for saying "That's all, folks!"? (Porky Pig)
  3. What five-and-dime store was famous across America? (Woolworth's)
  4. What did the milkman deliver? (Milk — right to the door)
  5. What singer was known as "The King" of rock and roll? (Elvis Presley)
  6. What did kids drop into a jukebox to play a song? (A coin / nickel)
  7. What comedy duo was famous for "Who's on First?" (Abbott and Costello)
  8. What was a popular drink at the soda fountain — a root beer what? (Float)
  9. Lucy and Ricky were the stars of what classic TV show? (I Love Lucy)
  10. What actor was known for saying "Well, hello there, pilgrim"? (John Wayne)

Finish the Phrase

  1. A penny saved is a penny... (earned)
  2. The early bird catches the... (worm)
  3. Don't cry over spilled... (milk)
  4. An apple a day keeps the doctor... (away)
  5. When it rains, it... (pours)
  6. Home is where the... (heart is)
  7. Actions speak louder than... (words)
  8. Better late than... (never)
  9. Every cloud has a silver... (lining)
  10. Absence makes the heart grow... (fonder)

How to run trivia so everyone wins

Read each question slowly, twice. Let the group answer together out loud rather than calling on individuals — chorus answering removes all pressure and doubles the volume of the fun. Celebrate every answer, including the wrong ones and the wonderful off-topic stories that questions about the old days will unlock. If a question lands flat, skip it without comment and move on. And bring more questions than you think you need: a lively group can go through 40 questions in twenty minutes, which is why our own trivia decks are built with 80 or more.
